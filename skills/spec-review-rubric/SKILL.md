# Spec Review Rubric

Use this skill when reviewing an idea, feasibility note, RFC, or implementation spec.

## Review Areas

1. **Problem framing**
   - Is the problem clearly stated?
   - Is the target user or stakeholder explicit?
   - Is the current pain measurable or evidenced?

2. **Scope**
   - What is in scope?
   - What is explicitly out of scope?
   - Are dependencies on other teams, systems, or data sources named?

3. **Assumptions**
   - Which assumptions are critical?
   - Which assumptions are unvalidated?
   - Which assumptions would break the proposal if false?

4. **Feasibility**
   - Are there technical constraints?
   - Are operational or staffing constraints ignored?
   - Does the proposal depend on unknown integrations, migrations, or approvals?

5. **Execution detail**
   - Are milestones or phases described?
   - Are ownership and handoffs clear?
   - Is there a rollback or fallback plan?

6. **Risk and rollout**
   - What could fail?
   - What would the blast radius be?
   - How would the team detect and mitigate failure?

7. **Decision quality**
   - What alternatives were considered?
   - Why is this option preferred?
   - What unanswered questions remain before execution?

## Output Format

Use this structure:

## Blocking Gaps
- Items that prevent confident implementation or approval.

## Major Risks
- Items that could derail the work or create rework.

## Ambiguities
- Unclear terms, ownership, requirements, or success criteria.

## Suggested Improvements
- Concrete edits or sections to add.

## Recommendation
- State whether the document is ready, nearly ready, or still exploratory.
